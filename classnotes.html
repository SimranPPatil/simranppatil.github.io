Fortifying the Four I's

  1. Identify: This concept revolves around finding the problem. To know if the problem is good, it should be engaging and relatable. Problems are mislabeled opportnities and empathising can help find these treasure troves. We need to keep observing and exploring and need to get out of the shell to identify!
  2. Ideate: Brainstorming and reflecting are two key features I believe. Juxtaposing ideas and putting curiosity to action can help build bold ideas. Think without any constraints. Build up the stockpile of knowledge. Diverge and then converge. Keep pivoting.
  3. Initiate: Taking the first step - be it research or prototyping - is important. Going out and testing the feasibility of the ideas are must. Team building, networking and assembling. Analysing the risk. From idea to a project. Setting milestones and having deadlines.
  4. Implement: Perservering. Put the research and ideation to work. Testing it, experimenting, getting feedback, developing the product. Just taking risk and heading out to make something. To shape the ideas to reality. If solution adds value to the user. was it worth it? Did we really solve the problem?
